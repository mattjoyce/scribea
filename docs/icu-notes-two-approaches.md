# Two Approaches to ICU Notes

*A teaching reference for registrars*

There are two common ways to structure an ICU note. They are not opposites — most experienced clinicians use both — but they ask very different things of the person writing them. Knowing which you are doing, and why, is part of becoming an ICU doctor rather than someone who completes ICU paperwork.

---

## Side-by-side

| | **Checklist approach** (A–Z, FAST HUGS, ABCDEF) | **Systems approach** (Neuro / CVS / Resp / Renal / GI / Heme-ID / Endo / Skin / Lines / Plan) |
|---|---|---|
| **What it is** | A fixed list of items you confirm for every patient, every shift. | An organ-system review where each section assembles that system's current story. |
| **Structure** | Linear and itemised. Each item is independent. | Hierarchical. Each system is a frame; the plan integrates across them. |
| **Cognitive demand** | Recognition. *"Have I addressed this item?"* | Construction. *"What is this system doing, why, and what moves next?"* |
| **What it protects against** | Omission. Forgetting DVT prophylaxis, head-of-bed, glucose control, etc. | Fragmentation. Treating findings in isolation without seeing what drives them. |
| **How it fails** | The note can be complete without the registrar understanding the patient. | The note can be eloquent but miss a routine item (the missed VTE prophylaxis). |
| **Best used for** | Daily safety net. Junior staff. Handover scaffolding. Audit. | Reasoning, prioritisation, presenting on rounds, communicating with the team. |
| **Question it answers** | *Did I do everything?* | *Do I understand what is going on, and what is the dominant problem?* |

## Same patient, both ways

> 68F, Day 3 ICU. Community-acquired pneumonia → septic shock. Intubated, on noradrenaline, oliguric AKI. Lactate trending down.

### Checklist note (FAST HUGS BID)

- **F**eeding — NG feeds, 30 mL/hr, tolerating
- **A**nalgesia — fentanyl infusion
- **S**edation — propofol, RASS −2
- **T**hromboprophylaxis — held (AKI, bleeding risk reviewed)
- **H**ead of bed — 30°
- **U**lcer prophylaxis — pantoprazole
- **G**lucose — VRII, BSL 7–9
- **S**BT — not today
- **B**owels — not opened, aperient charted
- **I**ndwelling devices — CVC, art line, IDC, all day 3, no signs of infection
- **D**e-escalation — pending micro

*Complete. Safe. But a colleague reading it cannot tell whether this patient is getting better or worse, or what we are worried about.*

### Systems note

- **Neuro** — Sedated on propofol, RASS −2, GCS not assessable. Lighten in AM with view to SBT.
- **CVS** — Noradrenaline weaning, 0.08 mcg/kg/min (was 0.25 on D1). MAP 70s, lactate 1.8 (peak 4.2). Continue weaning, off by tomorrow if trajectory holds.
- **Resp** — AC/VC, FiO₂ 0.4, PEEP 8, P/F 240. R basal consolidation improving on CXR. SBT tomorrow if off pressors.
- **Renal** — Oliguric AKI (Cr 280, urea 24, K⁺ 5.4). No RRT yet. Trigger for CRRT: rising K⁺, worsening acidosis, or fluid overload limiting respiratory wean.
- **GI** — NG feeds tolerated. Bowels not open D3 — aperient.
- **Heme-ID** — Hb 92 stable. Blood cultures grew *Strep. pneumoniae* — rationalise pip-taz to benzylpenicillin per micro. Day 3 antibiotics.
- **Endo** — VRII, target BSL 6–10.
- **Skin** — Early sacral pressure redness. Turning regimen, dynamic mattress in place.
- **Lines** — CVC / art line / IDC all D3. Reviewed, all stay. Reassess D5.
- **Plan** — Improving septic shock. Wean noradrenaline overnight, lighten sedation AM, target extubation in 24–48h. Renal function is the watch-item; if Cr/K⁺ climb, call before they cross threshold.

*A colleague reading this knows the patient is getting better, what the dominant problems are, and what would change the plan.*

## How to teach the move from one to the other

The checklist is the floor. Systems thinking is the ceiling. The teaching task is not to abandon the checklist but to stop the checklist *being* the reasoning.

A few levers:

1. **Force presentation by system on rounds, not by problem list or chronology.** This makes the trainee assemble each system rather than recite findings.
2. **Demand the causal story before the plan.** *"Tell me what is driving the AKI before you tell me what you're going to do about it."* The plan should fall out of the model, not the checklist.
3. **Run the checklist as a *post-hoc* safety pass, not the structure of the note.** Write the systems note. Then walk the checklist to catch omissions. This sequences cognition correctly: model first, audit second.
4. **Ask "what would change your plan?" for each system.** This converts a static description into a live model with thresholds.

---

*Checklist asks: did I do everything?*
*Systems asks: do I understand what is going on?*
*Both matter. Only one of them grows a registrar into a consultant.*
